# Ash IQ Brand Architecture

## Naming Roles

Ash IQ is the public-facing mobile app product.

Pine & Ash is the parent/source brand behind Ash IQ. It may be used as the brand source, ecosystem name, company-style signature, or origin story context, but the mobile app itself should not be called Pine & Ash.

Preferred public phrase:

```text
Ash IQ by Pine & Ash
```

Use this phrase when the audience needs brand context. Use `Ash IQ` by itself when the product name is already clear.

## Primary Tagline

Use:

```text
Know Your Smoke.
```

Secondary lines can support SEO or section context. `Your cigar palate, decoded.` may be used as an explanatory line, but `Know Your Smoke.` is the primary public website tagline.

## Public Website Role

The public website supports Ash IQ. It explains the product, shares the launch position, and points visitors to the waitlist or contact path.

The website is not the Ash IQ mobile app. It must not provide:

- Login or authentication.
- Website account creation.
- User profiles.
- Cigar logs.
- Saved cigars.
- Favorites.
- Want-to-try lists.
- Recommendations.
- Database-backed app functionality.
- Backend services.

Those experiences belong inside the future Ash IQ mobile app.

## Pine & Ash Role

Pine & Ash should support the product as the source brand. Good uses:

- `Ash IQ by Pine & Ash`
- About-page origin story.
- Footer ownership.
- Email/domain context.
- Brand and launch documentation.

Avoid using Pine & Ash as the app name in public copy.

Do not write:

```text
The Pine & Ash app
```

Write:

```text
Ash IQ
```

or:

```text
Ash IQ by Pine & Ash
```

## Private Tooling Boundary

The command center is private internal/admin tooling. It is not public-facing, is not the customer product, and should not appear as a product feature on the public website.

Public copy should focus on Ash IQ as the mobile app. If internal tooling must be referenced in developer documentation, keep it separate from marketing pages and clearly identify it as private.

## Copy Guardrails

- Do not imply Ash IQ is already downloadable unless app-store links are live.
- Do not imply website users can create app accounts.
- Do not describe discovery or recommendations as a current shipped feature before launch.
- Do not overpromise AI, machine learning, or algorithmic capability.
- Do not use `elevate`, `curate`, `unlock`, or `journey`.
- Do not use gendered framing.
- Do not position Ash IQ as a cigar blog, social feed, marketplace, retailer directory, coupon site, checkout flow, delivery flow, pickup flow, or where-to-buy service.
- Keep recommendations framed as app-based preference guidance, not sales or purchase routing.
